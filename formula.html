Nozzle Flow & Performance Analysis

1. Mach & Area Relations
Exit Mach \( M_e \)\( M_e = \sqrt{\frac{2}{\gamma - 1} \left[\left(\frac{P_c}{P_e}\right)^{\frac{\gamma - 1}{\gamma}} - 1\right]} \)
Area Ratio \( \epsilon \)\( \frac{A_e}{A_t} = \left(\frac{\gamma+1}{2}\right)^{-\frac{\gamma+1}{2(\gamma-1)}} \cdot \frac{\left(1 + \frac{\gamma-1}{2} M_e^2\right)^{\frac{\gamma+1}{2(\gamma-1)}}}{M_e} \)
2. Mass Flow Rate \( \dot{m} \)
General Flow\( \dot{m}_g = \frac{A_t P_c}{\sqrt{T_c}} \sqrt{\frac{\gamma}{R}} \cdot \left[M_e \left(1 + \frac{\gamma - 1}{2} M_e^2\right)^{-\frac{\gamma + 1}{2(\gamma - 1)}}\right] \)
Choked Flow (throat)\( \dot{m}_c = \frac{A_t P_c}{\sqrt{T_c}} \sqrt{\frac{\gamma}{R}} \cdot \left(\frac{\gamma + 1}{2}\right)^{-\frac{\gamma + 1}{2(\gamma - 1)}} \)
Continuity\( \dot{m} = \rho_t \cdot V_t \cdot A_t \)
3. Thermodynamic Properties (Stagnation & Throat)
Chamber density \( \rho_0 \)\( \rho_0 = \frac{P_c}{R T_c} \)
Throat velocity \( V_t \)\( V_t = \sqrt{\frac{2 \gamma R T_c}{\gamma + 1}} \)
Throat density \( \rho_t \)\( \rho_t = \rho_0 \left(\frac{2}{\gamma+1}\right)^{\frac{1}{\gamma-1}} \)
4. Exit Plane Properties
Exit Pressure\( P_e = P_c \left(1 + \frac{\gamma - 1}{2} M_e^2\right)^{-\frac{\gamma}{\gamma-1}} \)
Exit Temperature\( T_e = T_c \left(1 + \frac{\gamma - 1}{2} M_e^2\right)^{-1} \)
Exit Velocity\( V_e = M_e \sqrt{\gamma R T_e} \)
5. Performance Metrics
Total Thrust \( F \)\( F = \dot{m} V_e + (P_e - P_a) A_e \)    (\(P_a\) = ambient pressure)
Specific Impulse \( I_{sp} \)\( I_{sp} = \frac{V_e}{g} \)   (vacuum) ;   \( I_{sp} = \frac{F}{\dot{m} g_0} \) (sea-level)
Isentropic expansion assumed. For overexpanded/underexpanded conditions, shock losses may occur.

Bell Nozzle Geometry — Rao Method

QuantityFormula / Value
Throat radius \( r_t \)\( r_t = d_t / 2 \)
Expansion ratio \( \epsilon \)\( \epsilon = A_e / A_t \)
Throat arc radius \( R_1 \)\( R_1 = 0.382 \, r_t \)
Coordinates at point N\( x_n = R_1 \sin \theta_n ,\quad y_n = r_t + R_1 (1 - \cos \theta_n) \)
Convergent angle \( \theta \)\( 25^\circ \) (typical) , start angle \( \theta_n = 30^\circ \)
Exit angle \( \theta_e \)\( 8.5^\circ \) (optimum for short bell)
Rao length approximation\( L = f \cdot \frac{r_t}{\tan(15^\circ)} \left[ \sqrt{\epsilon - 1} + 1.5 \left(\frac{1}{\cos(15^\circ)} - 1\right) \right] \)   (f ~ 0.8)
Parabolic contour equation: \( r(x) = y_n + \tan(\theta_n)(x - x_n) + \frac{\tan(\theta_e) - \tan(\theta_n)}{2(L - x_n)} (x - x_n)^2 \)
Boundary conditions: \( r(x_n)=y_n,\; r'(x_n)=\tan\theta_n,\; r(L)=r_e,\; r'(L)=\tan\theta_e \)
Rao method provides a high-performance parabolic contour with minimized length while preserving thrust efficiency.
